Chicken Biriyani

Home

Description


Chicken Biriyani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It is made by layering marinated chicken with fragrant basmati rice, spices, and herbs, then cooking them together to create a rich and satisfying meal. The blend of spices such as cumin, coriander, cardamom, and cloves gives biriyani its distinctive taste and enticing aroma.

This dish is often served at celebrations and gatherings, enjoyed by people of all ages. Chicken Biriyani can be accompanied by raita, salad, or boiled eggs, making it a complete and hearty meal. Its unique combination of textures and flavors has made it a favorite in Indian cuisine and popular around the world.


Ingredients

  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 2-3 tablespoons biriyani masala (store-bought or homemade)
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-2 cinnamon sticks
  • 3-4 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Steps

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  5. Add the chicken pieces, yogurt, biriyani masala, mint leaves, and coriander leaves. Mix well and cook until the chicken is browned and cooked through.
  6. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained basmati rice, whole cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon sticks, and salt. Cook the rice until it is about 70% cooked (it should still have a bite to it).
  7. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture in the pot.
  8. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking.
  9. Once done, gently fluff the biriyani with a fork to mix the rice and chicken.
  10. Serve hot with raita, salad, or boiled eggs on the side.

Enjoy your delicious Chicken Biriyani!
